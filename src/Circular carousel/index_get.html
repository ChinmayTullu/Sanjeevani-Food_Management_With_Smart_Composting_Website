Global Food Waste

One-third of food is wasted globally: Around 1.3 billion tons of food are wasted every year, according to the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO). 25% of the world's freshwater supply is used to produce food that is never eaten.

Food Insecurity

Hunger persists amid waste: Nearly 828 million people globally suffer from hunger, yet a significant portion of food is wasted. Wasted calories: The food wasted globally could feed 3 billion people annually, reducing food insecurity.

Household Waste

Households are major contributors: In the U.S., households account for 43% of food waste, wasting about 30-40% of the food supply. Food waste per person: On average, each person wastes 121 kilograms (267 pounds) of food annually.
